Eric Schrijver is a Dutch interaction designer, artist and author, born in Amsterdam in 1984. He lives and works in Brussels.

Eric got his start as an author editing the blog I like tight pants and mathematics, which aims to motivate designers and artists to get more involved in the world of computer programmers. In 2018, he published his first book: “Copy This Book”, an artist’s guide to copyright, with Onomatopee. It’s been translated into Cantonese and French.

From 2011 to 2017 he was a core member of the graphic design collective Open Source Publishing.

Eric has taught workshops across Europe and in the U.S. He has been a teacher at the Masters Graphic Design at the École de Recherche Graphique (ERG), and as well as a faculty member at KABK (The Hague), where he taught coding and interaction design.
